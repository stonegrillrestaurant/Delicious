The Paradox of God
Main narration
--:--

We are told that God is perfect, yet creates what is flawed — a paradox that religion often avoids but the soul keeps returning to.

When we speak of a perfect being, we imagine a mind beyond error, a will beyond contradiction, and a love beyond condition. Yet the world we wake up into is stitched with accidents, cruelty, and silence. The more honest we are about experience, the more the words “perfect” and “loving” begin to tremble under the weight of reality.
