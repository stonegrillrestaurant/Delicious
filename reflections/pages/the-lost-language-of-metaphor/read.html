The Lost Language of Metaphor

A Solitude Reflection by Ninox Antolihao
On symbols, literalism, and the map that replaced the landscape.

Human beings once spoke to the deepest parts of life using metaphor. Not because they were weak thinkers — but because some truths cannot be held by literal sentences without becoming distorted.

Metaphor is not decoration. It is a bridge: from the visible world to the invisible meanings that shape the heart. A story can carry what plain instruction cannot.

But when metaphor is forgotten, people begin to worship the symbol instead of what it points to. The map becomes more sacred than the landscape. The instruction about love becomes more important than the actual feeling of love.

This is one reason religion can drift: not because the divine is absent, but because language becomes rigid. When symbols are treated as literal facts, they stop guiding the soul and start controlling the mind.

A living faith stays metaphor-literate: it remembers that the symbol is not the thing itself.
